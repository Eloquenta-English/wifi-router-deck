For Homeowners

Your Internet, Your Way

Why buying your own WiFi 6 router and choosing a separate LTE data contract saves money, stress, and headaches compared to taking the "free" router from your internet provider.

A straightforward guide for anyone who wants better internet without the fine print.

The Trap

The "Free" Router Isn't Really Free

Your internet provider gives you a router. It seems like a nice gesture. But here's what actually happens:

  • Monthly rental fee hidden in your bill — usually R50 to R150 per month, forever
  • Old, outdated technology — most ISP routers are WiFi 5 or older, struggling with modern homes
  • Locked settings — you can't improve coverage or fix problems yourself
  • You're stuck with them — changing providers means losing your router too

After 2 years, you've paid for the router twice over — but you still don't own it.

The Upgrade

What WiFi 6 Actually Means for You

Think of WiFi like a highway. Older WiFi 5 is a two-lane road. WiFi 6 is a six-lane motorway with smart traffic lights.

Old WiFi 5 (ISP Router)

congested
Phones, tablets, TVs, and laptops all fight for the same narrow lane. Video calls stutter. Netflix buffers.

New WiFi 6 (Your Router)

smooth
Handles 20+ devices calmly. Each device gets its own efficient lane. 4K video, video calls, and downloads all work at once.

WiFi 6 also reaches further through walls. That dead spot in the bedroom? Gone.

The Freedom

CPE LTE: Internet That Doesn't Need a Phone Line

CPE (Customer Premises Equipment) LTE is a fancy name for a simple idea: internet through the mobile network, straight into your home.

  • No landline required — works anywhere with cell signal
  • Same router, different source — your WiFi 6 router connects to a small LTE box instead of a cable
  • Competitive contracts — mobile data providers often charge less than fixed-line ISPs for the same speed
  • Move house freely — unplug, move, plug back in. No technician visits.

In South Africa and many EU countries, LTE home contracts now match fibre speeds at half the cost.

The Reality

"But I Can't Set It Up Myself"

Yes, you can. Modern routers are designed for people who are not IT experts. Here's what actually happens:

  • Step 1: Plug the router into the power and the LTE box (or fibre cable)
  • Step 2: Open the app on your phone — it finds the router automatically
  • Step 3: Choose a name and password for your WiFi
  • Step 4: Done. Your devices connect just like before.

If you can set up a WhatsApp account, you can set up a modern router. The hard part — cables, configurations, port settings — is handled automatically now.

And if you truly get stuck? A tech-savvy friend, a YouTube video, or the shop where you bought it will help in 10 minutes. You only do this once.

Your Move

Three Questions for Your ISP Tomorrow

1

"Am I paying a monthly router rental fee?"

Check your bill. Look for "router rental," "CPE fee," or "equipment charge." That's money you can stop spending.

2

"What speed is my current router?"

If they say WiFi 5 or "802.11ac," it's old. WiFi 6 ("802.11ax") is the current standard that handles modern homes.

3

"Can I use my own router with your service?"

Most ISPs say yes. If they say no, that's a red flag. A good provider lets you choose your own equipment.

Better internet, lower bills, and equipment you actually own. That's not complicated — that's just smart.
